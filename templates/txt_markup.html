Plain-text Markup Scheme

HANSEL uses a custom plain-text sceheme as its working format, developed in the Pramāṇa NLP project. It has two vertically separated parts: a Head above a long line of asterisks (*********) and a Body below (<head> and <body> elements in XML, respectively).

Body

The Body, containing the actual Sanskrit text, consists of three main types of elements:

  1. Structural markers, of two types:
    • - [milestone identifiers in square brackets], corresponding to paragraphs and verses (n attribute of <p> and <lg> elements in XML, respectively)
    • - {super-milestone identifiers in curley brackets}, corresponding to logical sections (n attribute of <div> elements in XML)
  2. Note markers, of two types:
    • - <tertiary structural markers in angle brackets> (<head> and <trailer> elements in XML
    • - (philological notes in round brackets i.e. parentheses) (<note> element in XML)
  3. Editorial additions and deletions
    • - (deletions in round brackets i.e. parentheses) — note that these are not distinguished from philological notes
    • - «Insertions in double-chevrons»

If all such elements are removed, what is left should be contiguous, valid Sanskrit natural-language text.

Head

The Head contains metadata with the following format:

  • - Triple-newline (\n\n\n) between fields
  • - Between these separators, fields beginning with the field name and a colon (e.g. “Title:”)
  • - Allowed fields include: Title, Author, Source, Extent, ...

These fields are parsed, validated, and mapped to corresponding elements in the XML <head> element for publication.
