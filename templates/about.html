About HANSEL

HANSEL is the companion to GRETIL, walking the same path, a step further on.

Like numerous other legacy collections online, GRETIL is still useful in its frozen state, but since it no longer has support to grow, we need a new place to accommodate new Sanskrit e-text material. HANSEL aims to the next collaborative space for high-quality Sanskrit material online. It is designed to be open and easy for students and scholars to contribute to. Contributed material may be upgraded forms of e-texts from previous repositories like GRETIL or SARIT, or it may be entirely new. Contributors are supported by a technical team consisting of skilled scholars who share the burden of maintaining both data and code.

"Human-accessible" means that material will always exist in forms that the greatest number of students and scholars can use directly, such as plain-text and HTML.

"NLP-ready" means that the data will also be structured in anticipation of computational applications, for those with natural-language processing ideas they'd like to try out on as much data as possible.

In addition to these two core values, HANSEL will also maintain a high standard of transparency, with clearly documented scholarly sources, open-source and versioned data and code, and easily searchable metadata.

Name and logo

The haṃsa (swan, or more accurately, bar-headed goose) symbolizes not only grace but also discernment (viveka). A world away, in the Grimms' tale, Hänsel and Gretel succeed against the odds by working together.
