About HANSEL

HANSEL is the companion to GRETIL, walking the same path together.

The goal of HANSEL is to continue the free and open exchange of academic-level Sanskrit e-texts. All items must clearly indicate their sources, and they also must eventually conform to basic structural requirements, but it's easy to get a project started. A technical team consisting of philologically and technically skilled scholars is ready to help you transform material and add it to the system. Whether you have brand-new material, upgraded versions of previous e-texts from GRETIL or SARIT, or even just high-quality image scans, simply get in touch by email.

Name and logo

"Human-accessible" means that material will always exist in forms that the greatest number of students and scholars can use directly, such as plain-text and HTML.

"NLP-ready" means that the data will also be structured in anticipation of computational applications, for those with natural-language processing ideas they'd like to try out on as much data as possible.

The haṃsa (swan, or bar-headed goose) symbolizes not only grace but also discernment (viveka). A world away, in the Grimms' tale, Hänsel and Gretel succeed against the odds by working together.
